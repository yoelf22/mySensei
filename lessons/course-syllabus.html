Course syllabus

"Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?" (Philip K. Dick) — the Christian themes inspiring the book, as opposed to its film-noir roots

Mercerism as a Christ myth: Wilbur Mercer as a Christ figure (the climb, the stoning, death and resurrection), the empathy box as communion and shared suffering, empathy as the line between human and android, the 'Mercer is a fraud yet still real' problem, Dick's Gnostic undercurrent, and how the novel's religious core is what the neo-noir film adaptation strips away.

Your path: 7 → 10 · 6 modules

  1. 1

    The Passion of Wilbur Mercer

    How Mercer's endless climb, the stoning, his death and rising map onto the Gospel passion narrative.

    → to level 8

  2. 2

    The empathy box as sacrament

    Fusion with Mercer as communion and imitatio Christi; the wounds that appear on the user's own body as stigmata.

    → to level 8

  3. 3

    Empathy as the line between saved and damned

    Mercerism's doctrine that empathy is humanity, why androids are barred, and what 'salvation' means in this religion.

    → to level 9

  4. 4

    "Mercer is a fraud" — and still real

    Buster Friendly's debunking versus Mercer still appearing to Isidore and Deckard; faith as true in its effects, not its facts.

    → to level 9

  5. 5

    Dick's Gnostic undercurrent

    World as prison (kipple, entropy, the fake and the real), salvation through hidden knowledge, and Mercer as a Gnostic rather than orthodox Christ.

    → to level 9

  6. 6

    Why the noir film had to cut it

    What Mercerism does that neo-noir can't carry, and the case that this is a religious novel wearing a detective's coat.

    → to level 10
